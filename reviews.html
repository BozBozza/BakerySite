TripAdvisor Reviews

"A step back in time - delicious bakery in Corfe"

esc014 - 17th August 2025

This little bakery in Corfe is like stepping into the 1950s – full of charm and tradition. They serve a variety of freshly made patties, all absolutely delicious and straight from the oven. Everything is warm, tasty, and sold at very honest prices. A true gem not to miss!

"Scrummy and Spoilt for choice"

DebbieGibbo1 - 20th October 2024

Visited the bakery to get something for lunch while visiting Corfe Castle. So much to choose from including hot sausage rolls, pie's and pasties. Cakes all looked delicious and very reasonably priced. We bought a Pork Pie, 2 huge Cheese Straws, a Vienesse Finger, Cinnamon bun and an Eccles Cake and cost a shy over £7.....very good price. We thoroughly enjoyed everything....the pastry on the pork pie was crispy and very tasty. I wish I lived in Dorset near this bakery as I could visit regularly. Fabulous little village bakery with amazing home made goods.

"Allergen friendly and delicious food"

Lau T - 20th September 2024

The highlight for me was the ingredient information readily available on each item in plain sight. Rare to find and extremely helpful to someone like myself with a severe nut allergy. More bakeries should lead by their example. Really nice staff and delicious baked goods from cakes to pastries and sandwiches - will be coming back!

"Best Bakery in Dorset"

Ashleigh - 10th August 2024

This bakery is amazing! We went back most mornings of our holiday to get bread for breakfast! We highly recommend the crusty rolls, but anything you go for is guaranteed to be great. Over the course of the week we tried the crusty rolls, cottage rolls, bacon/sausage baps, sausage rolls, cinnamon danish, milk choc chip cookie, croissants, etc. Everything was exceptional. (Just be warned that it’s so popular that parking near it is a bit trickier!)

"Very, very nice"

MCXV - 6th May 2024

Grabbed a quick pasty straight from the oven, and it was absolutely delicious. The pastry flaked, and it was just the right size. Only observation is that it was called a Cornish Pasty but had minced beef rather than cubed. Beware Trelawneys army fining out and laying siege to the shop! Doesn’t stop them being great though.
